BIOGRAPHY

Playboi Carti

2011–2016: Career Beginnings Carti started rapping in 2011 under the name Sir Cartier, later changing to Playboi Carti in 2013. His SoundCloud uploads gained attention, leading to collaborations with artists in Atlanta's rap scene. He toured with ASAP Ferg and Lil Uzi Vert and signed with Interscope Records in 2015. In 2016, he joined ASAP Mob's AWGE imprint. 2017–2018: Playboi Carti and Die Lit Carti released his debut mixtape in April 2017, receiving praise from various publications. The mixtape produced successful singles like "Magnolia" and "Wokeuplikethis". He toured with Gucci Mane and Dreezy and was featured on projects by ASAP Mob and Lana Del Rey. In May 2018, his album "Die Lit" debuted at number three on the Billboard 200 chart. 2019–2020: Whole Lotta Red After working on his second album "Whole Lotta Red" from late 2018, several of Carti's songs leaked online. He was featured on singles like "Pain 1993" by Drake. Carti released his first new music in 2020 with "@ Meh," followed by the announcement and eventual release of "Whole Lotta Red" on Christmas Day, 2020. The album debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200. 2021–present: Upcoming Album Carti continued featuring on tracks like those by Pi'erre Bourne and Lil 1 Dte. In 2021, he collaborated with ABRA and Kanye West. Carti hinted at a potential release in 2022 on Twitter. In 2023, he performed unreleased music at Rolling Loud California and teased collaborations with The Weeknd and Madonna. Carti also hinted at a collab album and performed new music at Wireless Festival. He confirmed touring plans with label artists in 2024 and was featured on Travis Scott's album "Utopia," hinting at his forthcoming third studio album.

Destroy Lonely

2015–2019: Beginnings Destroy Lonely started creating music at 14, recording initial tracks with friends Texaco and Nezzus. Together, they produced their first project, NezzusDestroyed, gaining traction with the 2019 track "Bane". 2020–2022: ᐸ/32 and No Stylist In September 2020, Lonely released his mixtape "ᐸ/3," followed by a deluxe edition. Playboi Carti noticed him after the release of "Oh Yeah" in December 2020. He signed with Carti's label, Opium, in early 2021. He appeared in Carti's "Sky" music video and performed at Lyrical Lemonade Summer Smash in April 2022. He contributed to Ken Carson's album and released his mixtape "No Stylist" featuring Ken Carson. He announced a tour, released a music video for "VTMNTSCOAT," and gifted Carti a ring for his birthday. 2023–present: If Looks Could Kill In November 2022, Lonely dropped a deluxe version of "No Stylist," titled NS+ (Ultra). He released music videos for "NEVEREVER" and "FAKENGGAS" in February 2023. Announced his upcoming album in April, he released the lead single "If Looks Could Kill" in March. In May 2023, he released his debut studio album, "If Looks Could Kill," featuring 25 tracks and a short film titled "Look Killa." In September 2023, he released a Director's Cut version with six new tracks. In October 2023, he featured on three tracks from Ken Carson's album "A Great Chaos."

Ken Carson

2015–2019: Beginnings Ken Carson joined 808 Mafia in 2015 as a rapper and started releasing music on SoundCloud in 2017. He gained attention in the underground rap scene, caught Playboi Carti's eye, and signed to his Opium label in 2019, being the first artist signed over Destroy Lonely and Homixide Gang. 2020–2022: Teen X, Project X, and X After signing with Opium, Carson dropped two EPs in 2020: "Boy Barbie" and "Teen X." In 2021, he released "Teen X: Relapsed" followed by his debut studio album "Project X." He appeared on Yeat's album "2 Alive" in 2022 and released his second studio album, "X," reaching number 115 on the Billboard 200. He also collaborated with SoFaygo on the single "Hell Yeah" and dropped a deluxe version of his "X" album titled "Xtended." 2023–present: A Great Chaos Carson released the single "I Need U" in February 2023 and featured on Destroy Lonely's album in May. He produced a track on Lil Uzi Vert's album in June. Carson announced his third studio album, "A Great Chaos," set to release on October 13, 2023. However, most of the album leaked online days before the release. He held an album launch party performing tracks from the album and had Playboi Carti as a guest performer.
